The clarity gap. And it's closeable.

The broken report. The CRM that nobody trusts. The AI tool that the team adopted but can't actually use. These look like configuration issues, or training gaps, or bad software choices. Underneath most of them is something simpler: people on different sides of a process don't have the same picture of what the work requires.

That's the clarity gap. And it's closeable.

I work with sales, marketing, and operations teams to find where the breakdown is actually happening — not where it's showing up — and fix it at the root. That means asking different questions than the ones that have already been asked. It means translating between what the business needs and what the systems can do. And it means sitting with the people doing the work long enough to understand what they're actually dealing with, not just what they've been able to put into a ticket.

This isn't a patch. It's a reframe of the problem, followed by work that holds.
