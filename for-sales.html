For Sales Teams

The data is usually fine. The picture it creates isn't.

Most sales problems are clarity problems. Let's find where the definition broke down.

Get in Touch

When revenue targets are slipping, the first question is almost always about the numbers — which ones are wrong, which reports to trust, which tool is failing. That's the right instinct, and it leads to the wrong diagnosis.

Most of the sales problems I work on are clarity problems. Imprecise ICP definition that looked fine in theory but doesn't hold up when it's operationalized in the CRM. Requirements that got translated into system configuration once, by someone who was approximating, and the approximation became the standard. Handoffs between sales and marketing where both sides are working from a different mental model of what a qualified lead actually is.

These don't look like communication failures. They look like system failures. But the system was built to spec — it's just that the spec was never clear enough.

Closing that gap means going back to where the definition broke down, getting the right people in the same room with the same picture, and rebuilding from there. The technical work that follows is usually straightforward. It's the clarity work that was missing.

If your team is doing everything right and the output still doesn't reflect it, that's worth looking at.
